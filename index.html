    Visual Attention Guidance Enables A Composable Brain-To-Image Decodin
    Sooyoung Kim, Joonwoo Kwon, Mincheol Park, Jeongwoo Seo, Won Woo Ro, Shinjae Yoo, Suhyun Kim, Yuewei Lin, Jiook Cha

    We have developed a new brain-to-image decoding model that allows composable prompt modulation using brain signals for the first time. Previous studies only leveraged the “nsdgeneral” brain region which is a subset of voxels in the posterior cortex most responsive to visual information. However, according to the two-streams hypothesis, the human brain processes visual stimuli across ventral and dorsal pathways. Therefore, we correlate them to ‘what’ and ‘where’ information in images to reconstruct what people see from functional MRI. We propose a unified brain encoding module for two visual pathways and a new framework that guides the model to the location and identity of objects from the brain based on diffusion models. Our brain-induced attention guidance directly modifies cross-attention layers of LDM at inference without further fine-tuning.
    AesPHA: An Aesthetic PHysics-Aware Neural Style Transfer
    Joonwoo Kwon, Sooyoung Kim, Shinjae Yoo, Yuewei Lin, Jiook Cha
    Planningo, Photio: AI-based product photography
    I am expected to serve as an AI researcher and consultant starting Oct. 2024, with an AI research partnership with Seoul AI Hub. I aim to advance an AI-based service Photio by resolving the incongruity between AI-generated backgrounds and original commercial products.

    We develop AI technologies that generate background images or videos corresponding to products. We consider the illumination and position of products for authentic outcomes. Furthermore, to advertise efficiently as an alternative to traditional promotional photography, we should provide various styles of backgrounds with short inference time. Our techniques are applicable in various domains such as advertising, art, film, and social media.
